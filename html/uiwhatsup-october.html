We are fortunate to have an active community within Recreational Services. This fall two new fitness programs are being launched: Bootcamp for First-Year Students and Abundant Bodies Yoga. Both programs are free, and you can learn more at recserv.uiowa.edu.
 
New Learn to Row classes are also being offered on Sunday, October 22. Participants will learn the basics of rowing on land and then try new skills in the Iowa River. No experience is necessary. Register at recserv.uiowa.edu/learn-to-row.
 
Intramural Sports are also in full force. From flag football to volleyball to spikeball and more, I hope you are getting involved.

Disability Awareness Month
 

October is also disability awareness month, and the Council for Disability Awareness is hosting a "I Am" poster campaign, and you will see fliers across campus. The CDA welcomes anyone on campus who would like to distribute fliers. 

The Hawkeye Accessibility Ambassador program kicked off another year with a training for new ambassadors on Friday, October 6. This program allows students with disabilities (and interested staff) the opportunity to advance their leadership and advocacy skills while concurrently assisting the University with designing facilities that are more universally designed, inclusive, welcoming and supportive of individuals with disabilities. 

Please contact Carly Armour for more information at carly-armour@uiowa.edu about either of these initiatives.

Suicide Prevention
 

September was National Suicide Prevention Month. I would like to take a moment to address suicide on college campuses. The numbers are staggering.
  • Suicide remains the #2 cause of death of college students in the US with approximately 1,200 deaths each year.
  • 1 in 12 students have made a suicide plan.
  • 1 in 10 students have considered suicide. 

Iowa is not immune. Please take care of yourselves and each other. If you notice a friend who is struggling with feelings of hopelessness, purposelessness, mood disturbances, anxiety, or feeling like there is no way out, help them get help. Remain calm and share your concerns. Listen carefully, validate their feelings and experiences, and encourage them to connect with University Counseling Service (UCS) or the National Suicide Prevention 24/6 Hotlines at 1-800-SUICIDE or 1-800-273-TALK.

Suicide prevention is serious business at the University of Iowa: asking can save a life!
 

Thinking of Las Vegas and Texas Tech University
 

On Monday, October 2, morning we awoke to news of the deadly shooting in Las Vegas. There are no right words for these tragedies, but I want you to be aware that the Office of the Dean of Students and University Counseling Services reached out to students from the Las Vegas area to check in and offer support. If you or others need assistance in processing the horrific tragedy, please don't hesitate to reach out. As details from this attack continue to emerge, let's keep those affected in our thoughts. 

Additionally, on Monday, October 9, our friends and colleagues at Texas Tech experienced a tremendous tragedy that resulted in the death of a campus police officer. Please keep the students, faculty, staff, and Lubbock community in your thoughts as well.
